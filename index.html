🦁 Meet Leo

Leo's Story

Leo is a lion cub just like you. This is his story — and it could be yours too.

1
Chapter One · The Problem

Leo walks alone...

Every morning, Leo walked sadly to school. His best friend was in trouble — something unfair had happened. Leo wanted to help but didn't know how. He didn't know that the law could protect them both.

Just like Leo, thousands of children across Sri Lanka face difficult situations every day — without knowing their rights or where to get help.

"I didn't know I had rights. I didn't know anyone could help me."
2
Chapter Two · The Discovery

Leo finds JusticeKids

One day Leo saw the JusticeKids board at his school gate. He scanned the QR code and discovered something incredible — he had rights. Real rights, protected by Sri Lankan law.

When Leo's friend needed help most, Leo pressed the SOS button. His location was sent to a trained field officer. Help was on the way.

"I pressed the button. Someone came. My friend wasn't alone anymore."
3
Chapter Three · The Change

Leo is not alone anymore!

The field officer helped Leo get legal support for his friend through the Legal Aid Commission. Leo learned all eight of his rights. He became a JusticeKids Rights Champion.

Now Leo helps other children learn their rights too. Because when you know your rights — you can protect yourself, and help protect others.

"My friend needed help. I found it. Now I want every child in Sri Lanka to know — help is always there."

📚 Word Glossary

What Do These Words Mean?

Legal words can sound scary. Here they are explained simply.

NCPA
National Child Protection Authority — a government body that protects children from abuse. Free helpline: 1929 (24 hours).
UNCRC
United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child — an international agreement signed by Sri Lanka giving all children specific rights.
Penal Code
Sri Lanka's main book of criminal laws. Hurting a child is a crime under the Penal Code.
Grama Niladhari
A government officer responsible for a village. They can help families in need and connect people to social services.
Legal Aid Commission
Provides free lawyers to people who cannot afford one — including children. Phone: 011 243 3618.
Juvenile Court
A special court for children under 16. Focuses on the child's wellbeing, not just punishment.
Medico-Legal Certificate
A document written by a doctor that records injuries. Can be used as evidence in court.
Zonal Director of Education
A government officer responsible for schools in your area. They can investigate complaints about schools.
